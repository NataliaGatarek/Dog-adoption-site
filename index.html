How to adopt the dog

Adopting a dog or cat means adding a new member to your family. As such, they need not only food and shelter, but also love and attention. Taking an animal from a shelter is not always easy in the beginning and you should give them some time to get used to you and your home.

Very often these animals do not know how to be „pets“ and you should be patient and give them a chance to learn and adapt. In return they will repay you with unconditional love, but you should never forget that you are responsible for that animal. He or she is not an object, a toy, or a temporary whim. You are all the world to them, and they rely on you for everything: food, shelter, attention, care, treatment when needed. Adoption is a serious step and should be carefully considered before taking the decision. If you have any doubts, please, contact us, and we will be happy to give you the right advice.

Herding Dogs

Herding dogs are one of the oldest types of working dog, assisting farmers with livestock.

Show more...Herding dogs have been bred for centuries to exhibit a specific form of predatory behavior that involves rounding up cattle and sheep by nipping at their heels. They are high energy, very intelligent and obedient, as they must be able to follow complex commands from their handler. An example of a common herding dog breed is a Border Collie..

Sporting Dogs

Sporting dogs are used as aids in human sports such as game shooting, where they are used to find, chase and retrieve game birds.

Show more...Typically small-medium, they are highly obedient and trainable, energetic and naturally alert, as well as excellent swimmers. They have good stamina that allows them to remain active throughout a long hunt, and as such require a lot of exercise even if kept as a pet. An example of a sporting dog breed is the American Water Spaniel, often used as a gun dog.

Working Dogs

Working dogs are dogs who are bred and trained to assist their human counterparts with daily activities. Show more...This category includes service dogs, sled dogs, guard dogs, police dogs, rescue dogs and farm dogs. Working dogs can be almost any breed but they are distinguished by their intelligence and high energy levels, and are usually on the larger side. An example of a common working dog breed is a German Shepherd working as a K-9 unit.
